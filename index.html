My grandfather and father came from rural Bangladesh. In the village, every household had a cow -- or wanted one. Rural children would spend most of their time with the cows, goats, or chickens. There was not much for the children to play with back then, so the animals were among the most essential things in their lives. They played with them, looked after them, and fed them. Children would get together and discuss the newborn calf or the hatching chickens. Animals were the centre of people’s lives
